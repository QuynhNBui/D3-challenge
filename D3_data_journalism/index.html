Demographic vs. Health Risk Factors

Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

In Poverty vs Obesity, Smokes and Lacks of Healthcare

There is a correlation between Poverty % and Health Risk Factos. With the increase in the Poverty %, the more people don't have healthcare. Similar to the correlation with Poverty, the Obesity% and Smokers% increase with the increase in the Poverty%.

Age vs. Obesity, Smokes and Lacks of Healthcare

The states with younger population have higher percentage of people who don't have healthcare. There is no direct correlation between age and Obesity% and Smoker %.

Income vs. Obesity, Smokes and Lacks of Healthcare

There is direct correlation between Household Income and Obesity, Smokes and Lacks of Healthcare. States with higher Household Income have lower Obesity %, Smokes % and Lacks of Healthcare. With higher income, people have more resource for their health care and better lifestyle.
